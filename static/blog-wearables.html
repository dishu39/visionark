Training Models for Wearable AR

Published in Development & AI Resources • 5 min read

Integrating complex Vision-Language models like BLIP directly into wearable hardware—such as Meta Smart Glasses—represents a massive leap forward in accessibility technology. However, it also introduces intense computational hurdles.

To achieve live, sub-second captioning for blind and visually impaired users, models cannot rely purely on heavy cloud servers, which introduce latency. Instead, we heavily focus on Model Distillation.

By training a smaller "student" network to mimic the detailed outputs of our massive "teacher" network, we condense the latent space boundaries without significantly dropping caption quality. We supplement this with highly specialized edge-case datasets containing POV (point-of-view) imagery, simulating what glasses actually see in dynamic environments, rather than standard, perfectly framed photography.
