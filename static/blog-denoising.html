How Denoising Improves Vision

Published in Algorithms & Image Processing • 4 min read

Autonomous vehicles face some of the most complex visual environments possible. Cameras operate in rain, snow, fog, and low illumination, inherently leading to massive amounts of visual noise. Feeding raw, corrupted pixels into a natural language descriptor network yields hallucinated text and dangerous misinterpretations.

Our newest algorithms automatically tackle this pre-inference. When a distorted dashcam image enters VISIONARK, our backend runs rapid signal-analysis to evaluate noise variance.

For Gaussian grain, we deploy Non-Local Means (NLM) heuristics. For harsh "salt and pepper" artifacting, a rapid Median-filtering matrix is applied prior to feature extraction. The result? The language model isn't trying to describe static; it describes the actual pedestrians and boundaries smoothly, saving crucial nanoseconds.
